Hard guarantees the legacy shell wrappers could not make:

- every step runs in its own process group with a hard timeout; a hung
  fetch is killed, recorded, and the job continues honestly
- a crashed run still writes its final status (try/finally) — no more
  ops_status stuck at "running" after a mid-run death
- a lock left behind by a dead process is detected (pid liveness) and
  taken over with a notification, instead of blocking every subsequent
  night until a human notices
- the run ledger is appended before the process exits, success or not
